Top three activities to do in Paris

Visit the arc de triomphe

Did you know you can climb 284 steps to the top of the Arc de Triomphe, commissioned by Napoleon, via underground stairs at the top of the Champs Elysées? At the base of the arch, the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier pays tribute to the 1.3 million French soldiers who died in World War I, where the eternal flame is rekindled at 6.30pm each evening.

Visit the Louvre Museum

Housing a vast collection of art spanning centuries and continents, the Musée du Louvre is a cultural treasure trove. It’s got a lot more in it than the little painting of Mona Lisa (which you can see from your couch thanks to live webcams) and Venus de Milo. You could literally spend days in here.

Visit Montmartre

From the iconic Sacré-Cœur Basilica to the bustling Place du Tertre, Montmartre offers a glimpse into Paris’ artistic soul and a chance to experience the city’s creative energy firsthand.
